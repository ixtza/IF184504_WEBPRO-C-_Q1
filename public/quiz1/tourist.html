Some places in Padang are known for its historical value and recreational destination.

Fort De Kock

Fort de Kock was a 19th-century Dutch sconce fortification established over a hill in Bukittinggi, West Sumatra, Indonesia. Around the fortification, a new settlement grew, which eventually grew into the city of Bukittinggi, the second largest city in West Sumatra. Although the remnants of the mound and some cannons can still be seen, the original buildings on top of the sconce has been demolished.

Pagarayu Palace

Pagaruyung Palace is a palace building which was used as a residence from the Pagaruyung royal family until 1833. The architecture of this building uses a typical Padang vernacular style so that it is similar to the Gadang House. Until now, Pagaruyung Palace has become one of the most popular tourist attractions in Padang. Here you can see there are lots of furniture and other Minangkabau relics which are certainly very antique.

ManijauLake

In Minang Language, Maninjau means “watching”, “observing”, or “take a look at”. Now this very large caldera lake is surrounded by super beautiful natural scenery accompanied by local resident houses. Of course you should not miss this unspoiled landscape as a tourist attraction in Padang that must be visited. In addition, you should try the sensation of playing paragliding on Lake Maninjau which is definitely very spectacular.

Mentawai Island

In the southwest of Padang City, there is a place that is very popular among surfers because of the waves and strong winds. If you like to surf, then you must come to the Mentawai Islands that has one of the big wave beaches in Indonesia.This tourist attraction in Padang is very well known among foreign tourists. This archipelago can be considered quite remote and even untouched until the 20th century. So the beauty of nature here is still very amazing to be enjoyed.
